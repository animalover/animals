Whales

Whales are magnificent creatures of the sea. Learn about their diet, predators, and more as you read this article.

Diet

Whales are divided into 2 groups: baleen whales and toothed whales. Baleen whales have soft pieces of "baleen" that are like sieves to filter through krill and sea plants from the water. Toothed whales have teeth that they use to chomp down on animals like squid and fish.

Predators

Whales do not have many predators, as they are so big. But sometimes sharks in a group will attack a whale that is by itself.

Breathing

Whales have blowholes, which are holes at the top of whales' heads. When whales rise to the water's surface to breath, a stream of water comes out of their blowhole. Some whales can hold their breath for up to 90 minutes!

Babies

Baby whales are called calves. When the calf is born, she drinks her mother's milk and plays with other calves. The mother has to push the calf up to the surface so it can breathe. Calves usually stay with their mother for a year, then they go off to find themselves a mate.

Threats

Whales are endangered because of many things. Some boats use drag net to catch fish, but whales can be caught in them. Whalers illegally harpoon whales. And yet other people litter and it pollutes the ocean, harming whales and many other sea creatures. Lots of whales are endangered, sadly.

Help the Whales!

You can do a lot to help whales!

  • wear a save the whales shirt
  • adopt a whale to help with whale research
  • pick up people's litter so it won't get to the ocean
  • encourage people not to litter
  • tell other people about whales!

Fun Facts

  • all dolphins are whales, but not all whales are dolphins
  • the biggest animal on earth today is the Blue Whale
  • scientists tag whales to keep track of them
  • a Blue Whale's heart beats 5 times per minute
  • an elephant could sit on some whales' tongues!
